# Babagigi Language Roadmap (v1 — Cartographer)

**Source artifact:** `../08-artifacts-archive/babagigi-language-roadmap.jsx`
**Status:** Superseded by `babagigi-demand-roadmap.md` but historically significant

---

## What this was

The first attempt at visualizing Babagigi's language expansion across waves. Cartographer aesthetic — designed to feel like discovery and mapping rather than aggressive expansion.

## Why it was rebuilt

The first version organized waves by what felt right intuitively. The rebuilt version (`babagigi-demand-roadmap.md`) anchored each wave to specific demand signals: speaker counts, age demographics, partnership readiness, tech readiness. Demand-first organization made the strategic logic legible to investors and partners.

## What was retained from v1

The wave structure (1-4), the principle of mission-track work being cross-subsidized, the visual treatment of language families and diaspora geography. The cartographer aesthetic informed all subsequent visual work.

## What changed in v2

- Demand signals (age 60+ percentage, US heritage speaker count) made primary axes
- Commercial vs. mission track distinction made explicit toggle
- Tagalog elevated to Wave 1 based on demographic urgency
- Cantonese separated from Mandarin
- European heritage languages (German/Italian/Polish) moved down despite high speaker counts
